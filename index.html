Paragraph 2

Use of minor sentences (short sentences)

  • The minor sentence shows that he is speechless/overwhelmed so can only express himself in a fragmented way as he takes it all in.

Paragraph 3

Examples:

  • Simile: Flashing on and off, rising and falling like electronic
  • Effect: Captures the virancy of the scene but also its unnatural, human-made qualities

  • Use of commas/list: pictures symbols, graphics etc.
  • Effect: Listing what he can see ads to the effect that he is being visually overloaded/exposed/bombarded

  • Short sentences: A whole world of neon (Hyperbole)
  • Effect: expresses amazement.

  • Elipsis: Flashing on and off, rising and falling like electronic, coloured rain...
  • Effect: Suggest that there is more.

  • Complex sentence: Flashing on and off...
  • Effect: seem very fragmented reflecting the chaotic jumble of things he is taking in
