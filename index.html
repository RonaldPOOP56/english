Tokyo by Graham Marks

Vocabulary

  1. Disorientated: confused about where to go
  2. Dithering: Wasting time, not making a decision

Paragraph 2

Use of minor sentences (short sentences)

  • The minor sentence shows that he is speechless/overwhelmed so can only express himself in a fragmented way as he takes it all in.

Paragraph 3

Examples:

  • Simile: Flashing on and off, rising and falling like electronic
  • Use of commas: Every color of the rainbow, except the subtle ones.
  • Short sentences: A whole world of neon
  • Use of lists: pictures, symbol, graphics.
  • Elipsis: Flashing on and off, rising and falling like electronic
  • Complex sentence: And the occasional English words, just to add the confusion.
